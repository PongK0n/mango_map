# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Thai and international visitors — people curious about or planning a trip to บ้านป่าเหมี้ยง (Ban Pa Miang), a low-carbon highland community in Lampang, Thailand. They arrive wanting to understand what the village is, picture themselves there, and take an action (explore the map, browse travel packages). Some are Thai travelers planning a domestic eco-trip; others are international tourists discovering sustainable Thai community tourism. Both groups come to be moved by the place before they decide to go.

## Product Purpose

MIANG MAP is the digital front door for Ban Pa Miang's community tourism. It tells the story of a 200-year-old highland village that lives with the forest — harvesting Miang tea leaves, growing shade-grown arabica coffee, and practising low-carbon hospitality. The site's job is to make the village feel real and worth the journey, then guide visitors to the interactive map and travel packages. Success looks like: visitors feel the place before they arrive, and leave confident about booking or planning.

## Brand Personality

Warm, cultural, story-driven. The brand voice is that of a knowledgeable local guide — unhurried, proud without being boastful, deeply rooted in Lanna traditions and the rhythms of the forest. The emotional goal: visitors should feel the cool mountain air, smell the tea leaves steaming, and sense a community that genuinely lives what it offers.

## Anti-references

- Loud adventure / extreme-sports aesthetics: neon colors, aggressive typography, high-adrenaline energy — the opposite of this village's spirit.
- Generic AI landing pages: cream/sand/beige backgrounds, identical card grids with floating emoji icons, tracked uppercase eyebrow labels on every section, hero-metric stat boxes.
- Overly corporate eco-tourism or greenwashing vibes: polished stock photography, buzzword-heavy copy (seamless, world-class, next-generation sustainable travel).
- Generic Tourism Authority brochure style: government-template layouts, informational-first over story-first, flat color blocks.

## Design Principles

1. **The village speaks first.** Imagery and language carry the experience; UI is the frame, not the painting. Every layout decision should make the village's own textures, sounds, and stories feel closer.
2. **Uniquely Thai, not generically tropical.** Draw from Lanna visual culture, Thai graphic design traditions, and the aesthetic of highland northern Thai communities — not the international eco-tourism template.
3. **Story before specification.** Lead with feeling and narrative; facts and logistics follow. The map and packages are the destination, but the story is the path.
4. **Functional warmth.** The site has real tools (interactive map, package browser). Design should make those feel as warm and intentional as the brand — not like a drop-in from a generic travel booking app.
5. **Honest simplicity.** Low-carbon living is unpretentious. The interface should carry that same quality: nothing decorative that doesn't earn its place, nothing that performs sophistication over substance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text). No specific declared user needs, but the bilingual Thai/English nature of the content means typography choices must support both scripts legibly. Thai script requires taller line-height and careful font selection to remain readable at body sizes.
